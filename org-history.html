Student-Led Cloud-First Community-Driven AWS Builder Center

AWS SBG AdU is Adamson University's home for cloud builders — a student-led chapter of the AWS Student Builder Group, connected to a global network of peers learning, certifying, and shipping real projects on AWS.

Who We Are

Volunteers from across Adamson who believe cloud skills should be open to everyone — not locked behind expensive courses or intimidating jargon.

What We Do

Hands-on labs, certification prep, hackathons, and mentorship circles that turn curiosity into deployable skills on EC2, S3, Lambda, and beyond.

Why It Matters

Every workshop, project, and study session builds a pipeline of Adamsonian builders ready for internships, competitions, and careers in the cloud.

"By the students, for the students, and with the students" — that is the heartbeat of everything we run, from our first lab to the community we are still growing today.

How a small idea grew into a community

What began as a handful of cloud-curious Adamsonian students has grown into one of the university's most active tech communities — a place to learn, build, and belong.

Every milestone on the right marks a step in our mission to make AWS and cloud computing accessible to everyone, one workshop at a time.

Milestones
2024

The Spark

A small circle of students came together to make AWS approachable on campus — and launched our first hands-on labs in Cloud Foundations, EC2, and S3.

2025

Growing the Community

Membership crossed 100. Mentorship circles, study groups, and certification prep became part of our weekly rhythm.

2026 · Today

A Cloud Family

AWS SBG AdU is a welcoming home for builders — "by the students, for the students, and with the students" — and we're just getting started.
